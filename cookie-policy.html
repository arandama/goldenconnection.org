1. What Are Cookies?

A cookie is a small text file that a website stores on your computer or mobile device when you visit. Cookies help websites remember information about your visit, like your preferred language or text size, which makes your next visit easier and the site more useful.

What cookies do:

  • Remember your preferences and settings
  • Help websites understand how many people visit
  • Allow certain website features to work properly

What cookies DON'T do (on our site):

  • Track you across different websites
  • Show you personalized advertising
  • Collect sensitive personal information
  • Access files or data on your device

3. How to Manage Cookies

3.1 Using Our Cookie Banner

When you first visit our website, you'll see a cookie consent banner with these options:

  • Accept All: Enable analytics cookies to help us improve the site
  • Decline: Only use essential cookies (the site works perfectly without analytics)
  • Cookie Settings: Learn more and customize your choice

Your choice matters: Declining analytics cookies won't affect your ability to use the website. All features, content, and services remain fully accessible whether you accept or decline.

3.2 Changing Your Mind

You can change your cookie preferences anytime:

  • Click "Cookie Settings" in the footer of any page
  • The cookie banner will reappear, allowing you to change your choice
  • Your new preference will take effect immediately

Our website respects "Do Not Track" (DNT) browser signals. If you enable DNT in your browser settings, we will automatically disable analytics cookies even if you haven't explicitly declined them in our cookie banner.

If you use your browser's private or incognito mode, cookies are automatically deleted when you close the window. In private mode, you'll see the cookie banner each time you visit because your previous choice isn't remembered.
