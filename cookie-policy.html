Cookie Policy

Effective date: 7 Nov 2025

This page explains the types of cookies we set on your device, why we set them, and how you can control them.

1. What is a cookie?

A cookie is a small text file that a website stores on your browser. Cookies let the site remember information (such as your language preference or whether you have accepted the cookie banner) across different pages and visits.

2. Cookie categories we use

Category Purpose Examples (name) Duration
Essential Required for the site to function (session handling, consent storage). PHPSESSID, cookie_consent Session / 1 year
Analytics Collects anonymous usage statistics so we can improve the site. _ga, _gid, _gat 2 years / 24 h / 1 min
Functional (Maps) Remembers your preference for Google Maps (e.g., language, zoom level). CONSENT 2 years

3. How we obtain consent

On your first visit you will see a cookie‑consent banner powered by Klaro. You can:

  • Accept all – enables essential, analytics and maps cookies.
  • Accept selected – you can keep analytics or maps disabled.
  • Reject all – only essential cookies are set.

Your choice is stored in the cookie_consent cookie for one year. You can change your preference at any time by clicking the “Cookie Settings” link that appears in the banner footer.

4. Managing cookies yourself

If you prefer to manage cookies directly in your browser, you can:

  • Open your browser’s privacy/settings panel.
  • Delete or block individual cookies (search for the names listed above).
  • Enable “Do Not Track” – our analytics script respects this signal and will not set analytics cookies.

5. What happens if you disable non‑essential cookies?

Disabling analytics means we won’t collect usage statistics, which may limit our ability to see which pages need improvement. Disabling the Maps cookie will cause the embedded map to reload with default settings each time you visit.

6. Changes to this Cookie Policy

We may update this page when we add new cookies or change existing ones. The “Effective date” at the top shows the latest revision. Any significant change will trigger the banner to appear again so you can review the new options.
