Privacy Policy

Effective date: November 7, 2025

Last updated: November 7, 2025

Simple Summary: Golden Connection collects minimal information (just your name and general location when you contact us) to provide free technology education services to seniors. We never sell your data, we don't track you across the web, and we only use analytics to count visitors and improve our website.

1. Who We Are

Golden Connection provides free technology education to adults (primarily 55+) in smaller communities across the United States, with a focus on rural areas.

Organization Status: 501(c)(3) nonprofit status pending. Federal tax-exempt application has been filed with the IRS and is currently under review.

Service Area: Nationwide (we travel to communities to provide in-person education)

Contact:
Email: hello@goldenconnection.org
Website: www.goldenconnection.org
Mailing address will be added when our 501(c)(3) status is finalized.

2. What Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

When you contact us through our website contact form or email, we may collect:

Information Type What We Collect Why We Need It
Name Your first name (or full name if you provide it) To address you personally in our response
Email Address Your email address To respond to your inquiry or service request
General Location City/county or general area (e.g., "rural Pennsylvania") To determine if we can serve your area and for impact reporting
Message Content Whatever you write in the contact form or email To understand and respond to your questions or needs

What we DON'T collect: We never ask for or collect Social Security numbers, financial information, health data, precise GPS location, passwords, or other sensitive personal information.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we automatically collect limited technical information:

Data Type What It Is Purpose
Website Analytics Number of visitors, pages viewed, time on site, general geographic region To understand how many people use our site and which pages are most helpful
Device Type Whether you're on a phone, tablet, or computer To ensure our website works well on all devices
Browser Information Type of web browser (Chrome, Firefox, Safari, etc.) To fix technical issues and improve compatibility
IP Address Your device's internet address (anonymized after 24 hours) For security, fraud prevention, and general regional statistics

2.3 Browser Storage (Font Size Preference)

Our website allows you to adjust text size for easier reading. When you change the text size, we save your preference in your browser's local storage (not a cookie). This data stays only on your device and is never sent to us.

How to clear it: Clear your browser's local storage, or use the "Reset" option on the font size controls.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for these purposes only:

  • Respond to inquiries – Answer your questions about our services
  • Arrange services – Schedule classes or instruction sessions in your area
  • Improve our website – Fix bugs, improve navigation, and create better content
  • Measure impact – Count how many people we serve (required by potential funders/sponsors)
  • Legal compliance – Meet record-keeping requirements for nonprofit operations

What we NEVER do: We never sell your information, rent your information to third parties, use your data for advertising, track you across other websites, or share your data with marketers. Your information stays with us.

4. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

4.1 Analytics Cookies

We use Google Analytics (or similar service) to understand how visitors use our website. This helps us improve the site and measure our reach.

What Google Analytics collects:

  • Number of visitors and page views
  • Which pages are most popular
  • General geographic location (city/region level, not your exact address)
  • Device type and browser used
  • How you found our website (Google search, direct link, etc.)

Your choices:

  • When you first visit, you'll see a banner asking for consent to use analytics cookies
  • You can decline analytics cookies and still use the entire website
  • You can change your choice anytime by clicking "Cookie Settings" in the website footer
  • You can install the Google Analytics Opt-out Browser Add-on

4.2 Essential Cookies

We use one essential cookie to remember your cookie preferences (whether you accepted or declined analytics). This cookie is necessary for the cookie consent system to work and doesn't require separate consent.

4.3 No Marketing or Advertising Cookies

We do not use advertising cookies, social media tracking pixels (like Facebook Pixel), or any third-party marketing technologies. We don't show you ads, and we don't let others track you on our website.

For more details, see our Cookie Policy.

5. How Long We Keep Your Information

Data Type Retention Period Reason
Contact form submissions 12 months To respond to inquiries and maintain service records
Service participation records 3 years Required for nonprofit reporting and grant compliance
Analytics data 26 months (Google default) To track long-term website trends
IP addresses (full) 24 hours Security and fraud prevention only
Font size preference Indefinitely (in your browser only) Until you clear browser storage or change devices

After the retention period, we permanently delete your information from our systems. We do not keep "backup" copies after deletion.

6. How We Protect Your Information

We take data security seriously:

  • Encryption: Our website uses HTTPS/TLS encryption for all data transmission
  • Access controls: Only authorized volunteers/staff can access contact information
  • Secure storage: Data is stored on password-protected, encrypted systems
  • No credit cards: Since our services are free, we never collect payment information
  • Regular reviews: We review our security practices regularly

Data breach notification: In the unlikely event of a data breach affecting your personal information, we will notify you via email within 72 hours of discovering the breach.

7. Sharing Your Information

We do NOT sell, rent, or trade your personal information. Ever.

We may share limited information only in these specific situations:

7.1 Service Providers

We may share data with:

  • Email service: To send responses to your inquiries (e.g., Gmail, email hosting provider)
  • Website hosting: Technical providers who host our website infrastructure
  • Analytics: Google Analytics (to count visitors and improve the site)

These providers are bound by contract to protect your data and can only use it for the specific service we contracted.

7.2 Aggregated/Anonymized Data

We may share general statistics with funders, partners, or the public (e.g., "We served 150 people in rural Pennsylvania last year"). These statistics never include names, contact information, or anything that could identify you personally.

7.3 Legal Requirements

We may disclose your information if required by law or to:

  • Comply with a subpoena, court order, or legal process
  • Protect our legal rights or property
  • Prevent fraud or abuse of our services
  • Protect the safety of our participants, staff, or the public

7.4 Business Transfer

If Golden Connection merges with another nonprofit or transfers its programs to another organization, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction. We would notify you before this happens.

8. Your Privacy Rights

You have the following rights regarding your personal information:

Right What It Means How to Exercise
Access Request a copy of the personal data we have about you Email us at hello@goldenconnection.org
Correction Request that we fix inaccurate information Email us with the correct information
Deletion Request that we delete your information (subject to legal limits) Email us with your deletion request
Opt-out Decline analytics cookies and tracking Use the cookie banner or change settings in the footer
Data portability Receive your data in a portable format Email us requesting your data

How to contact us: Email hello@goldenconnection.org with "Privacy Request" in the subject line. We will respond within 30 days.

Note: Some information must be retained for legal or operational reasons (like nonprofit reporting requirements). If we cannot delete certain data, we'll explain why.

9. Children's Privacy

Golden Connection's services are designed for adults (primarily age 55+). We do not knowingly collect personal information from anyone under 18 years old. If we discover we have collected information from someone under 18, we will delete it immediately.

If you are a parent or guardian and believe we have collected information about your child, please contact us at hello@goldenconnection.org.

10. Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to external websites (for example, links to Google Maps for directions, or links to helpful tech resources). When you click these links, you leave our website and are subject to those websites' privacy policies.

We are not responsible for the privacy practices of other websites. We encourage you to read the privacy policies of any website you visit.

11. California Privacy Rights (CCPA)

If you are a California resident, you have additional rights under the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA):

  • Right to know: What personal information we collect, use, and share
  • Right to delete: Request deletion of your personal information
  • Right to opt-out: We don't sell personal information, so this doesn't apply
  • Right to non-discrimination: We won't discriminate against you for exercising your rights

We do not sell personal information. We have not sold personal information in the past 12 months, and we will not sell personal information in the future.

To exercise your CCPA rights, email hello@goldenconnection.org.

12. European Privacy Rights (GDPR)

While Golden Connection primarily serves U.S. residents, if you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR):

Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data based on:

  • Consent: When you contact us or accept analytics cookies
  • Legitimate interests: To provide services, improve our website, and measure our impact
  • Legal obligations: To comply with nonprofit reporting requirements

Your GDPR Rights

  • Right to access your data
  • Right to rectification (correction)
  • Right to erasure ("right to be forgotten")
  • Right to restrict processing
  • Right to data portability
  • Right to object to processing
  • Right to withdraw consent
  • Right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority

To exercise your GDPR rights, contact us at hello@goldenconnection.org.

13. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices, services, or legal requirements. When we make significant changes, we will:

  • Update the "Last updated" date at the top of this page
  • Post a notice on our website homepage for 30 days
  • If we have your email, send you a notification of material changes

We encourage you to review this Privacy Policy periodically. Your continued use of our website after changes are posted means you accept the updated policy.

Golden Connection is committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal information with respect and transparency. We believe technology education should be accessible, safe, and trustworthy for everyone.
